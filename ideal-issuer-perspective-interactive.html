Issuer vs PSP-Merchant Architecture

INBOUND HUB vs OUTBOUND PSP · CLOUD SQL MR vs ALLOYDB · SCA ENGINE vs REDIRECT · SCHEME CERT vs PSP API KEY

💡 Key architectural insight: The issuer receives inbound requests from the Hub — not from a customer browser. There is no redirect chain. The SCA happens inside your systems. The debit is your responsibility. You are on the other side of the irrevocable guarantee that the merchant receives.

Issuer Architecture

  • Inbound: Hub sends payment initiation over mTLS
  • State store: Cloud SQL Multi-Region (strong consistency across regions)
  • SCA: your engine, your push notification, your biometric
  • Debit: your ledger, your responsibility, your idempotency key
  • Settlement: you send SCT Instant to PSP collection IBAN
  • Cert management: scheme certificates must never expire
  • Failure impact: your outage = your customers cannot pay anywhere
  • Regulation: licensed bank / EMI, DNB oversight, PSD2 Art.97

PSP-Merchant Architecture

  • Outbound: you call PSP API, Hub routes to bank
  • State store: AlloyDB / Cloud SQL (sufficient for merchant volumes)
  • SCA: happens inside the issuing bank — you have zero visibility
  • Debit: PSP handles — you receive a webhook after the fact
  • Settlement: PSP sends settlement file (T+2 typical)
  • Key management: PSP API key and webhook secret
  • Failure impact: bank outage → redirect 503 → implement circuit breaker
  • Regulation: PCI DSS scope, GDPR, no banking licence required
